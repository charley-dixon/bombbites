Teriyaki Tofu Bao Buns

These buns were an absolute home run. This is easily one of my favorite dishes I have ever made, and quite an enjoyable process as well! The light and pillowy buns came together with a few simple pantry ingredients. In this post I will walk you through the process of making the buns from scratch.

Falafel

I have not made falafel very often because it seemed to turn out dense and dry, but I discovered a technique that I had not yet tried that I was eager to it test out! This technique includes adding whipped egg whites to the batter to produce a lighter, airy texture. Although the batter seemed wetter than we expected, we were thrilled with the outcome. These little falafels were light, crisp and heavenly. Join me this week in a Mediterranean feast!

Green Chile Guacamole

This dip is fully loaded, so if you are a guac purist, this is not the recipe for you. But if you’re looking to kick it up a notch with your guac, I think you’ll want to give this one a spin. The base of the recipe is my go to guac, but I kicked it up a notch this time with some extra flair. The holiday weekend is just around the corner and this is could be your perfect pool party pleaser!

Detroit Style Pizza

As my parting goodbye to the children’s museum, I made 18 pizzas for the staff to thank them for crushing this crazy year. A coworker asked if I could teach her how to make pizza before I left so we made everything from scratch. This is my grandpas dough recipe and my uncles sauce recipe.
